A Navbar That Gets Out of Your Way

Scroll down and the navbar slides away. Scroll back up and it reappears instantly — smooth, smart, and distraction-free.

Scroll down

Why Smart Navbars Matter

Traditional fixed navbars permanently occupy precious screen real-estate, especially on mobile devices. A smart navbar solves this by dynamically showing and hiding based on the user's scroll direction, maximising the content viewing area without sacrificing quick access to navigation.

This pattern is widely used across modern applications — from Medium and Google Chrome's mobile tab bar to countless progressive web apps — because it strikes the perfect balance between accessibility and immersion.

How It Works Under the Hood

The implementation is deceptively simple: a single scroll event listener compares the current window.scrollY against the last recorded position. If the delta is positive (scrolling down), a CSS class is toggled that applies transform: translateY(-100%) — sliding the navbar upward out of view. When the delta reverses, the class is removed and the navbar smoothly transitions back into position.

Edge-case handling ensures the scroll position is clamped to zero to avoid negative values caused by elastic (bounce) scrolling on macOS and iOS. The entire script weighs in at fewer than 20 lines of vanilla JavaScript — no frameworks, no dependencies.

Design Principles

The glassmorphism styling uses backdrop-filter: blur() to create a frosted-glass effect, allowing underlying content to subtly show through while maintaining legibility. The dark palette centres around deep navy tones with indigo and purple accent gradients.

Micro-interactions — like the underline that grows on hover, the rotating logo icon, and the floating scroll-hint — add a layer of polish that makes the interface feel responsive and alive without being distracting.

This extra content exists purely to give you more room to scroll and experience the navbar behaviour in action. Try scrolling down slowly, then quickly flick upward — the navbar snaps back into view with a satisfying slide.
